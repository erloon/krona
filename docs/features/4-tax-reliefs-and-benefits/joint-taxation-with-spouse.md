# Functional Specification: Joint Taxation with Spouse

## 1. Executive Summary
The "Joint Taxation with Spouse" (Rozliczenie z małżonkiem) functionality allows B2B contractors in Poland to simulate and optimize their net income by applying joint tax settlement rules. Under Polish tax law, spouses filing jointly can pool their incomes, which is particularly beneficial when there is a significant income disparity between them (e.g., one spouse earns above the first tax bracket threshold, while the other earns below it or has no income). The core goal of this feature is to calculate the adjusted Personal Income Tax (PIT) liability, accurately reflecting the mutual tax-free allowance and shared tax thresholds, ultimately providing the user with a precise projection of their optimized monthly and annual net B2B earnings.

## 2. Input & Configuration Parameters
| Configuration Option | Available Values / Settings | Functional Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Taxation Form (Forma opodatkowania) | Tax Scale (Zasady ogólne / Skala podatkowa), Flat Tax (Podatek liniowy), Lump Sum (Ryczałt) | Joint taxation is legally permitted ONLY when the Tax Scale is selected. If Flat Tax or Lump Sum is selected, the joint taxation feature must be disabled or hidden. |
| Joint Taxation Toggle | Yes / No | Activates the joint taxation calculation module and reveals the spouse's income input fields. |
| Spouse's Annual Income (Dochód/Przychód małżonka) | Numeric value (PLN) ≥ 0 | Used to determine the combined tax base of the household. If set to 0, the user gains the maximum benefit from the spouse's unused tax-free allowance and lower tax bracket. |
| Spouse's ZUS Contributions (Składki ZUS małżonka) | Numeric value (PLN) ≥ 0 (Optional, depending on calculator depth) | Deducted from the spouse's gross income to accurately determine the spouse's individual tax base before pooling. |

## 3. Core Functional Logic
* **Availability & Validation Rule:** The application actively listens to the "Taxation Form" state. The Joint Taxation toggle is structurally bound to the "Tax Scale" (Zasady ogólne). Changing the form to 19% Flat Tax or Lump Sum immediately resets and disables the Joint Taxation toggle.
* **Tax Base Consolidation:** * The system calculates the primary user's taxable income (Revenue - Operating Costs - Social ZUS Contributions).
    * The system calculates the spouse's taxable income (based on user input).
    * The system aggregates both values to form the *Combined Household Tax Base*.
* **Joint Tax Calculation Algorithm (Polski Ład):**
    * **Step 1:** Divide the *Combined Household Tax Base* by 2 (creating an average individual tax base).
    * **Step 2:** Apply the standard Polish tax scale to this halved amount:
        * Calculate 12% tax on the amount up to the first threshold (120,000 PLN).
        * Subtract the tax-free allowance reducing amount (3,600 PLN per person, which equates to the 30,000 PLN tax-free base multiplied by 12%).
        * Apply 32% tax on any surplus above 120,000 PLN.
    * **Step 3:** Multiply the resulting tax figure by 2 to establish the total PIT liability for the household.
* **Health Contribution Isolation:** Under the "Polski Ład" (Polish Deal) reforms, health insurance contributions (Składka zdrowotna) are strictly individualized. The calculator logic MUST compute the primary user's 9% health contribution based solely on their individual income, completely ignoring the joint taxation model. Joint settlement impacts PIT only, not health contributions.
* **Pro-rata Monthly Distribution:** Since B2B calculators usually display monthly breakdowns, the system allocates the total annual tax liability across 12 months, smoothing out the "jump" to the 32% tax bracket that the primary earner would normally experience later in the year.

## 4. Output & Reporting Metrics
| Output Metric | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| Adjusted Income Tax (PIT) | The recalculated advance income tax amount (zaliczka na podatek) for a given month or the entire year, significantly lowered due to the shared tax-free allowance and thresholds. |
| Net Income (Na rękę) | The final B2B take-home pay, recalculated to include the tax savings generated by the joint settlement. |
| Effective Tax Rate | A dynamically updated percentage showing the actual tax burden relative to the gross income, reflecting the blended rate achieved through joint taxation. |
| Tax Savings Projection | (Optional but recommended) A calculated delta showing the exact financial benefit in PLN gained by choosing joint taxation over individual settlement. |

## 5. Business Rules & Constraints
* **Tax Form Exclusivity:** Statutory Polish law mandates that joint taxation is strictly prohibited if either spouse conducts business activity taxed via Flat Tax (Podatek liniowy 19%) or Lump Sum (Ryczałt ewidencjonowany), even if the other spouse uses the Tax Scale. The calculator assumes the primary user is on the Tax Scale; if they switch to an unsupported form, the calculation must instantly revert to individual rules.
* **Tax-Free Allowance Limits (Kwota wolna od podatku):** The algorithm inherently applies a pooled tax-free allowance of 60,000 PLN (2 x 30,000 PLN).
* **First Tax Bracket Expansion:** The 12% tax bracket is effectively stretched from 120,000 PLN to 240,000 PLN for the household. The 32% rate only triggers when the *combined* income exceeds 240,000 PLN.
* **Zero Income Scenario:** If the spouse's income is explicitly left blank or set to 0, the calculation proceeds validly. The system treats the spouse as having 0 PLN income, applying their full 30,000 PLN tax-free allowance and 120,000 PLN threshold entirely against the primary user's income.
* **Solidarity Tax Exclusion (Danina Solidarnościowa):** Joint taxation calculation does NOT circumvent the 4% solidarity tax. If the primary earner's individual income exceeds 1,000,000 PLN, the 4% solidarity surcharge must still be calculated based on their individual income, not the halved joint base.
